Iceland

Iceland is a country of sharp contrasts. A place where fire and ice coexist. Where dark winters are offset by the summer's midnight sun. A country whose insular existance has spurred a rich and vibrant culture.

When making a trip to ICeland, it is hard not to pay attention to the country's namesake-namely, its4,500 square miles of glaciers. Ice climbing on the glaciers is practised year-round and takes place mainly on the Glaciers in the south, to which day trips are offered from the Reykjavik anf Skaftafell.
